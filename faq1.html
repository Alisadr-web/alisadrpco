Student FAQs

Question 1: What is ICT?

In simple words, ICT stands for Information & Communication Technology. It trains students to become capable professionals such as programmers, data scientists, or backend developers by combining programming, networks, databases, and systems thinking.

Question 2: Who can study ICT?

Anyone who enjoys finding problems and solving them can study ICT. Curiosity, persistence, and a willingness to learn are more important than prior experience.

Question 3. How can you scan a document and send it to your laptop at HZ?

You can simply scan a document at HZ by following the instructions via HZ website, there are useful inforamtions.

Question 4: What will happen after graduating in ICT?

After graduating in ICT you can work in many types of companies and roles: software developer, backend engineer, data scientist, system analyst, DevOps engineer, or other related positions. Many graduates also continue with specialisations or master programmes.

Question 5: How can you print a document from your laptop at HZ?

If you want to scan or print a file, you can use the printers located in HZ buildings. Usually you authenticate using your student card — check local instructions or ask service staff if unsure.
